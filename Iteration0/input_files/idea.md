**Title**: Benchmarking Structural Inequality: A Comparative Analysis of Absolute Progression and Institutional Selectivity Gaps (2009/10–2022/23)

**Description**: This research quantifies the 14-year evolution of equity gaps in English HE, distinguishing between overall progression rates and selective "high-tariff" access. We define the "Conversion Ratio" as the proportion of HE-enrolled students entering high-tariff providers, applying Wilson binomial confidence intervals to mitigate volatility in small-cohort subgroups like Looked After Status and SEN provision. To ensure analytical robustness, we perform a sensitivity analysis by aggregating sparse cohorts where necessary, preventing noise-driven outliers from skewing trend estimates. We calculate the absolute progression gap (Q1–Q5 POLAR) and the conversion disparity, fitting simple linear period-average slopes with 95% confidence intervals to measure the magnitude of change. The 2020/21–2022/23 period is explicitly flagged as a high-volatility window influenced by non-standard assessment practices, ensuring these years are treated as descriptive observations rather than indicators of long-term structural shifts. This dual-metric framework isolates whether demographic disparities are primarily driven by barriers to initial HE entry or by systemic exclusion from selective institutional pipelines.
