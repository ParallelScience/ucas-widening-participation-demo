**Title**: Univariate Equity Benchmarking and Relative Selectivity Analysis in English Higher Education (2009–2023)

**Description**: This study quantifies educational inequity by calculating the Relative Risk Ratio (RRR) of progression rates for each `breakdown_topic` against their respective reference cohorts (e.g., POLAR Q5) to standardize comparisons across disparate group sizes. To isolate barriers to selective entry, the research employs a "Relative Selectivity Score," which normalizes the high-tariff progression rate of a group against the national average, mitigating the noise inherent in raw conversion ratios. The longitudinal analysis utilizes a segmented regression model with a structural break dummy for the 2020/21 academic year to isolate policy-driven trends from COVID-19-induced volatility. While restricted to univariate analysis, the research identifies "compounding disadvantage" by ranking the magnitude of equity gaps across dimensions, revealing which characteristics exhibit the greatest resistance to parity. By comparing the RRR of overall progression against the Relative Selectivity Score, the analysis distinguishes between groups facing systemic exclusion from all HE versus those disproportionately excluded from high-tariff institutions. This framework provides a robust, policy-relevant metric for evaluating the efficacy of widening participation initiatives over the 14-year period.
